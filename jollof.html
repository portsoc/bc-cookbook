Nigerian Jollof Rice

Note: This recipe is Nut-Free, Dairy-Free, and avoids Red Meat.

Jollof rice is a legendary West African dish. The secret to a good Nigerian Jollof is the "party" flavourâ€”a slight smokiness achieved by letting the bottom "burn" just a little.

Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 45 mins | Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 3 cups Long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup Vegetable oil
  • 1 Large red onion, finely chopped
  • 3 tbsp Tomato paste (puree)
  • The Blend: 3 Red bell peppers, 1 Scotch bonnet (habanero), and 3 large tomatoes (blended until smooth)
  • 2 cups Chicken or Vegetable stock
  • 1 tsp Thyme and 1 tsp Curry powder
  • 2 Dried bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Base: Heat the oil in a large pot. Add the chopped onions and fry until translucent. Stir in the tomato paste and fry for about 5 minutes on medium heat until it loses its sour tang.
  2. The Sauce: Add the blended pepper and tomato mixture. Cook down on medium-high heat for 10-15 minutes until the water has evaporated and the sauce has thickened/darkened.
  3. Seasoning: Stir in the thyme, curry powder, bay leaves, and stock. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  4. The Rice: Rinse the rice thoroughly under cold water to remove excess starch. Add the rice to the pot. The liquid should just barely cover the rice (about 1cm above). If you need more, add a splash of water.
  5. The Steam: This is the most important part. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid (or foil then the lid) to trap the steam. Turn the heat down to low.
  6. Simmer: Cook for 20-30 minutes. Do not stir it constantly! Once the rice is tender, turn up the heat for the last 2 minutes to get that signature "smoky" aroma from the bottom.
  7. Serve: Remove the bay leaves, fluff with a fork, and serve hot.
